# Skill: Email Proposal

## Description
Use this skill when writing and sending the business proposal to a prospected lead — a presentation email for the new version of the site, with rapport and no pricing.

## Objective
Make the client CLICK the link to the new page. If they open it and the page is good, they reply — and the sale happens in the conversation, not in the email.

## Golden Rules

### 1. NO PRICING
Pricing in the first email becomes mental spam — the client dismisses before opening the link.

### 2. Real rapport first
Open with something specific and verifiable:
- Google rating
- Number of reviews
- A detail mentioned in reviews
- Real credential (residency, specialization)
- **Never** generic praise

### 3. Gentle flaw mention
Cite 1-2 objective points about the current site, framed as opportunity:
- "I noticed the site doesn't reflect the level of your work"
- "I noticed the site isn't mobile-friendly"

### 4. The delivery is the argument
"I took the liberty of preparing a new version of your site" + published link.
It's a version of THEIR site, not a quote.

### 5. Light CTA
Just ask them to open it and share their thoughts. No artificial urgency, no "limited spots."

### 6. Keep it short
120-180 words. Busy professionals don't read long emails from strangers.

## Email Structure

| Part | Content |
|------|---------|
| **Subject** | Specific. E.g.: "A new version of Dr. Ana's website" |
| **P1 - Rapport** | Who found you + specific praise |
| **P2 - Observation** | 1-2 points about the current site |
| **P3 - The delivery** | "I prepared a new version, already live" + link |
| **P4 - CTA** | "Open it on your phone too and let me know what you think" |
| **Signature** | Name, presentation, WhatsApp from config |

## Example

```
Subject: A new version of Dr. Ana's website — 4.9★ on Google deserves more

Hello Dr. Ana,

I found your practice on Google Maps and was impressed by your 87 reviews and 4.9 rating — that's work that clearly delivers exceptional results.

I noticed your current website doesn't reflect that level of excellence: it's not mobile-responsive and the contact number is hidden in the footer, which might be causing you to lose patients who try to reach you from their phones.

I took the liberty of preparing a new version of your site, more modern and already mobile-optimized, which is live at this link:
https://mysite.com/clients/ana-nutri/

If you could open it on your phone and let me know what you think, I'd really appreciate it. I made sure to keep your practice's identity — they're just the improvements your work deserves.

Best regards,
John Smith
Digital Presence Specialist for Healthcare Professionals
WhatsApp: +55 11 99999-9999
```

## Sending

- **Draft** (recommended): provide the email for the user to copy and send
- **Direct**: if the assistant has the capability and authorization
- **Always** attach/indicate a screenshot of the new page to increase click-through rate

### Leads without email

If the lead doesn't have a confirmed email:
1. Adapt the message for WhatsApp (80-100 words)
2. Use the same content: rapport → observation → new page link → CTA
3. WhatsApp link: `wa.me/55DDDNUMBER`
4. Register in `leads.md` as "proposal via WhatsApp"
5. Suggest the user send the message manually via WhatsApp

## Follow-up

After 3-4 business days with no response, 1 single polite follow-up:
"Hello [Name], did you get a chance to look at the new version of the site? Any questions, I'm available."
